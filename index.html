Executive Summary

The story shifted this week. After a hawkish June FOMC — Chair Kevin Warsh’s first meeting (Jun 17), which held at 3.50%–3.75% and produced a dot plot with nine of eighteen officials seeing another hike — the June jobs report (Jul 2) undercut the hawkish case. Payrolls rose just +57k (vs ~115k expected), and April–May were revised down a combined 74k, dragging the 3-month average to only ~78k. Odds of a September hike fell to roughly 50% from ~64% the day before.

The soft print reframes the core tension rather than resolving it. Unemployment actually fell to 4.2% — but for the wrong reason: participation dropped to 61.5% (lowest since 2021) as people left the labor force, and the household survey showed 507k fewer employed. This is the hard data finally bending toward the weak soft data (sentiment, LEI) the dashboard has flagged for weeks. Meanwhile the ACM 10-year term premium remains positive (~0.7%), so the long end still demands duration/fiscal compensation even as front-end hike bets unwind — the 10Y eased only modestly to ~4.49%.

Liquidity stays the quiet swing factor, though its cushion is thinning: M2 continues to grow (~$23.05T), bank credit is expanding, and reserves remain ample (~$3.1T) — but the ON RRP facility has now drained to just ~$545M, effectively exhausted as a shock absorber. Credit spreads have ticked modestly wider off their tights (HY OAS ~270bp, up from ~263bp), a small but notable shift rather than continued compression. The gap between still-easy financial conditions and the Fed’s restrictive stance — now with a fading liquidity buffer added to the mix — is the central thing this dashboard monitors.

Things to Watch

Cross-pillar risks and divergences that could move the regime read in either direction.
Policy

Fed caught between mandates

Warsh stayed hawkish on inflation, but the soft jobs print cut Sep-hike odds to ~50%. A cooling labor market plus sticky 4%+ inflation is the classic stagflation bind — the end-July FOMC is now genuinely two-sided.

Rates

Term premium mean-reversion

ACM term premium at ~0.7% vs a ~1.4% historical median. A drift back toward normal implies ~70bp of further upward pressure on the 10Y, independent of Fed cuts — a duration risk for 60/40.

Credit

Spreads at record tights

HY OAS in the bottom decile leaves no cushion. A 2020–22 refi wall for CCC issuers meeting higher-for-longer rates is the slow-burn default risk.

Banking

CRE × regional banks

The known fault line. Office maturities at higher rates drive regional-bank losses; watch KRE relative performance and CRE delinquencies for the realized-loss signal.

Growth

Hard data is starting to bend

The Jul 2 jobs report (+57k, 74k of downward revisions) is the first real crack — soft data may be winning the divergence. Watch July ISM and the Aug 7 jobs report to confirm whether this is the turn.

Fiscal

Supply meets the term premium

High deficits + Fed runoff + fading foreign demand force the private sector to absorb long-dated paper. The quarterly refunding mix and auction tails are the early-warning system.

Currency

Yen weakness & carry unwind

A multi-decade-low yen vs a hawkish Fed raises BoJ/MOF intervention risk; a sharp carry unwind transmits globally to credit and equities fast.

Liquidity

TGA rebuild & RRP drain

Net liquidity (balance sheet − TGA − RRP) is the quantity that reaches markets. A TGA rebuild with a near-empty RRP would drain reserves directly — a quiet tightening.

Inflation

Breakeven de-anchoring

Breakevens at ~2.2% vs realized CPI 4.2% — the market calls it transitory. A 5y5y forward break above ~2.5% would say expectations are slipping, forcing the Fed’s hand.
